The road to become a CMSE® – Certified Machinery Safety Expert (TÜV NORD) was rough.  
This certification isn't easy, though I don't plan to demotivate you — rather, I want to share my experience.

I had to deep dive into harmonized ISO standards under 2006/42/EC. Understanding the difference between somewhat similar standards such as IEC 61508 and ISO 13849 was a challenge on its own.

After months of studying standards, the time came: the training itself.  
It's an 8‑hour, 4‑day training. You go over a thousand slides, and after that, you have to answer a test.

Can you imagine?  
It's an open‑book, 1‑hour exam — so you figure, it's tricky.  
The wording is confusing because the core idea of the test is to check how deeply you really understood the standards, the situations where each applies, and the reasoning behind them.

Once you're done, you hand in your exam, and 3–4 weeks later you get an email.  
And it's only **“pass / no pass”**.  
No further explanation, no feedback, no indication of what you got wrong. Nothing.

So if you fail, better luck next time.  
And let me tell you this: when I was there, besides me, there were 18 people taking it for the second time.

So my advice is simple: **study, study, study**.  
And when you get there, take notes.  
They hand you post‑its — use them all. Mark everything.
